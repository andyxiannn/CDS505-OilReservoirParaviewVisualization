Introduction

The following assignment focusses on building a simulation model of an oil reservoir with 2D and 3D data collected over the span of 50 years. The simulation model is to give us a representation of the various pressures of oil and water present in the reservoir to address the issue of effective oil and gas extraction

Defining the problem

An oil and gas reservoir is when oil and natural gas is trapped within a rock formation. To extract this, traditional drilling and pumping methods are used. The area it is used in is called an oil well, the ideal placement of which is a challenge. While drilling it is important to understand and measure the pressure and saturation of the oil and water trapped within [1].

The oil and gas industry are the backbone of many country’s economies. The extraction of oil and gas comes with its own set of challenges. One such challenge is the position of the extraction wells. Wells must be placed in optimal positions to extract the maximum oil and gas within the specific region. Data collected from an oil reservoir of the water saturation and pressure of oil and water help petroleum engineers to conduct analysis and comprehend it. This method which is cost effective and has less risk enables engineers to gain insight to place wells for optimal extraction.

1. How does the three measurements vary overtime in the simulated domain?

Initially, the water pressure, water saturation and oil pressure will increase together. 

Once the water saturation becomes constant, the water pressure and oil pressure will continue to increase. 

Finally, oil pressure will decrease while water pressure remains constant.

The observation is consistent on both 2D and 3D data.

They discovered the decrease in oil permeability at any given water saturation will result in water breakthroughs at lower water saturations and higher water-oil ratios for a given water saturation following breakthrough than if gas were not present [2].

4. What advantages/disadvantages did you find when using ParaView to visualise this dataset?

     The advantages of paraview is it’s designed for visualising large data sets in parallel which can process upto 250 Million unstructured dataset and 6 billion of structured cell [3]. Parallelism is supported by ParaView, either through shared-memory processes or through MPI-based distributed-memory processes. Data is automatically read, processed, and rendered in a data parallel manner when ParaView is run in parallel. The rendering module of ParaView supports both degree of detail and parallel rendering approaches, allowing for interactive rendering of very large datasets.

Importing Data

     Roger and Albert mentioned the measurement of changes in the physical and fluid conditions of an oil and gas field over time is known as 4D reservoir monitoring. As a result, it combines cased-hole logging, permanent down-hole sensor insertion, 3D seismic re-shoots, production history matching, and reservoir simulation to provide a near-real-time picture of what's going on inside the reservoir. As the oil field ages, the picture of fluid dynamics underground is updated on a regular basis, making the reservoir simulation more accurate [4]. 

     The 2D data and 3D data are processed separately. All dimensional related datasets were first merged. Then, added a temporal field;month. Next, the 2D data were added with spatial column of x and y. For the  3D data spatial column of x, y and z are added to each of the lines.

        Finally, the 2D data and the 3D data are sliced into 600 individual files in separate directories. By slicing the data into 600 time slices, Paraview will be able to load the csv as a group which will be interpreted as 1 frame.
